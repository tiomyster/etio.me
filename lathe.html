DESKTOP LATHE

MIT Spring 2013

2.72 - Elements of Mechanical Design

overview

In this class I learned to design a desktop lathe using custom and sourced parts. With my 5 person team, I used a combination of mathematical modeling, computational simulations, and experimentation to achieve physical robustness and reliable cutting. At the end of the semester, our lathe was put to a death test and survived a 1 meter drop and the weight of an adult on its crossfeed.

As the machining and modeling guru for my team, I was heavily involved in justifying our design choices and fabricating machine components.
