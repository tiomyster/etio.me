MIT Fall 2013

2.S994 - Biomimetics, Biomechanics, and Bio-inspired Robots

summary

The goal of this project was to investigate the effects of changing physical parameters of a tennis swing on the motion of the ball. The system is modeled as a 2 DoF system consisting of 2 links, each independently controlled by a respective torque. The robot is made out of 3D printed and controlled using an mbed microcontroller. My partner and I used MATLAB simulations to optimize arm torque, torso torque, and their timing patterns. We then used PD control to command a series of angles to the torso and arm to replicate the MATLAB simulations. By adding springs to the wrist of the robot, we observed how changing wrist compliance affected ball trajectory.

I was responsible for modeling our system and writing the MATLAB simulation for optimization. I was also heavily involved in writing the code for the mbed.
