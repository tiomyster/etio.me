Places like public playgrounds are overridden with bacteria and viruses. One way to keep kids healthy is to encourage good hand hygiene and frequent hand washing/sanitizing. Current hand sanitizing products are boring and unattractive to kids.

As part of a product exploration exercise of this class, I worked on a product called Spray, an interactive hand sanitizer for kids. I worked with a team of 8 to create works-like models to model how Spray would grab attention and activate the spray. We also made looks-like models to explore different ways kids could interact with the product.

Benchmarking - Researched products similar to spray to understand our competitors and define our product.

Rapid Prototyping - Designed and fabricated the looks-like models with blue foam.

User Testing - Tested the looks-like models on potential users at a park to understand which forms and interactions were most appealing to kids.
